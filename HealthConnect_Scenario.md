# Scenario:
You are hired by HealthConnect Clinic (a private medical practice) to conduct a white box penetration test on their Electronic Health Records (EHR) training server, which is hosted in a VM-based environment. The rest of the clinic’s production systems, including patient-facing portals and internal HR systems, are strictly off-limits:

Authorization Details:

-  You may simulate attacks on the VM-based EHR Training Server only, which is a Ubuntu Server 16.04.4 LTS (192.168.20.50).

- Only the IT administrator is aware; doctors, nurses, and staff are unaware.

- Social engineering is permitted, including phishing or fake software update attacks, provided no real patient data is compromised.
